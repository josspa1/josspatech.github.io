PocketBudJet · Beta Tester Guide

Welcome, tester.

Build 105+ Updated: April 29, 2026 Feedback: support@josspatech.com
The deal: You're getting an early build of PocketBudJet — a personal-finance app that runs on your device and never asks for your bank credentials. We need real-life use to surface the rough edges. The list below tells you what to try, what to ignore, and how to send feedback.

1. Install

iPhone: Tap the TestFlight invite from your inbox. If TestFlight isn't installed yet, the App Store will prompt you. Accept and tap "Install" inside TestFlight.

Android: Tap the Internal-Testing invite from Google Play. The Play Store opens straight to PocketBudJet — tap "Install."

Updates land automatically. If you ever feel like you're seeing yesterday's app, force-quit and reopen.

2. Your first ten minutes

Goal: get the app to the point where it has enough of your data to feel useful.

  1. Run through Setup. The wizard asks for your monthly income source(s) and a few common categories. Skip anything that doesn't fit — you can edit later.
  2. Add 5–10 recent transactions. Easiest way: tap the big + on the dashboard and dictate or type them. Or scan a receipt with the camera.
  3. Open the dashboard. See if the cards make sense for your situation. They should look like your spending, not a demo.
If anything in the first 10 minutes confused you, that's the most valuable thing you can tell us. Confusion in the opening minutes is a bug, even if everything technically worked.

3. Hidden gems we want stress-tested

These features are built and we want eyes on them. Some are buried — that's part of what we're trying to fix.

Calendar bills view

See what hits your account this week, next week, this month — all on one calendar grid.

Find it: Budget tab → Bills & Recurring → Bills tab
Net Worth over time

One chart of your assets minus debts going back as far as you have data. Useful even if you only enter a starting balance.

Find it: Reports → Net Worth History
Subscription audit

The app sniffs out recurring charges (Netflix, gym, cloud storage) and shows them all in one place with totals. We want to know whether it found yours and whether anything looked wrong.

Find it: Budget tab → Bills & Recurring → Subscriptions tab
Receipt gallery

Every receipt you've scanned, browsable as a grid with date and category filters. Tax-season superpower.

Find it: Transactions → Receipt Gallery
Mileage log

Log business / medical / charity miles with the IRS rate auto-applied. Rolls into the tax export.

Find it: Reports → Mileage Log
Tax export

One-button export of deductible transactions + Schedule C-style buckets + your mileage. Email it to your CPA.

Find it: Reports → Tax Export
Email forwarding

Brand new. Forward bank/billing emails to a private address; PocketBudJet parses them into transactions for your review. Off by default — flip it on in Settings if you want to try.

Find it: Import → Email Forwarding
Couples / household view

Track shared finances with a partner. Different from Partner Sync (Bluetooth-based device sync) — this is the household-level dashboard.

Find it: Settings → Couples Dashboard
Investment portfolio

Track 401k / IRA / brokerage balances over time. Manual entry — we don't read your accounts.

Find it: Settings → Investment Portfolio
Home-screen widgets & smartwatch tile

Glance at your top number from your phone home screen or watch face. Setup instructions inside the app.

Find it: Settings → Widgets & Smartwatch
Notification preferences

Single screen with every alert type (overspend warnings, bills due, weekly recap). Tune to taste — don't just disable everything in the OS.

Find it: Settings → Notification Preferences

4. What we want to hear

  • "This was confusing." Specifics please — which screen, what you expected, what you got.
  • "This crashed." What you tapped right before it died. Bonus: a screenshot of the error.
  • "This was wrong." Wrong number, wrong category, wrong date. Tell us what should've shown up instead.
  • "I couldn't find X." Even if X exists — that's a finding.
  • "This was great." Don't skip this. Knowing what's working is just as valuable.

5. What we're NOT testing in this round

  • Bank login / Plaid. Not in this build. Manual entry, receipts, PDFs, and email forwarding are the import paths.
  • Credit-score tracking. Coming in a later version.
  • Pricing / subscription billing. Pro features are unlocked for testers.

6. Sending feedback

Three ways, in order of preference:

  1. TestFlight (iOS): shake the phone or use the in-app feedback button → goes straight to us with a screenshot and crash log.
  2. Email: support@josspatech.com — describe the issue and tell us what device + OS you're on.
  3. Texting Joe: if you have his number and it's urgent, fine. We'll log it.
Privacy reminder. Your financial data lives on your phone. Don't include account numbers, balances, or other private detail in feedback unless we ask. Screenshots with numbers blurred are fine.

7. Thank you

Tester rounds are how an app stops feeling like the developer's pet project and starts feeling like something real people use. The honest, awkward, "this didn't make sense" feedback is the gold here. Send it. We'd rather hear it now than hear it from a 1-star review.
