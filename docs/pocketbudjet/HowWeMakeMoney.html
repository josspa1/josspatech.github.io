Trust & Transparency

How We Make Money

Updated April 2026
The short version: we charge a small subscription. We don't sell your data, run ads, take referral kickbacks, or share your transactions with anyone. If we did, we'd say it here.

Subscriptions are the only revenue

PocketBudJet is built and maintained by JosspaTech, a one-person studio. The app is free to start. Some advanced features — deeper reports, AI assistance, longer history, and bigger tax-export windows — are part of an optional monthly or annual subscription.

That subscription is our only source of revenue. There is no other business model behind it. No data brokerage, no advertising, no commission for steering you toward any product or service.

What we don't do

  • We don't sell your data. Your transactions, balances, goals, and notes never leave your device unless you explicitly turn on a sync feature, and even then they go only to storage you control.
  • We don't run ads. No banner ads, no sponsored placements, no “recommended cards” that pay us a finder's fee.
  • We don't take kickbacks. When the app suggests a budget category, refinance check, or savings move, that suggestion is based on your numbers — not on which company paid us.
  • We don't read your data to train models. AI features run on aggregated, anonymized patterns or on your device. Raw transaction details aren't fed into any training pipeline.

Why this matters

The standard playbook in personal-finance software is “free app, monetize the data” — either by selling aggregated user behavior to financial institutions, or by selling lead-gen referrals back to the user (offers for credit cards, refinance products, brokerages). That model creates an incentive to know you better in order to monetize you better.

We chose the simpler path: charge enough that the app pays for itself, and don't go fishing for second revenue streams. It's a smaller business. It's also a more honest one.

Aggregate, anonymized data. When you opt in, the app contributes anonymized aggregate signals (e.g., “most-shared category names”) to a shared crowd-sourced reference that helps everyone categorize transactions faster. It contains no amounts, no merchants tied to you, and no personal identifiers. You can opt out in Settings.

Pricing transparency

Our subscription pricing is shown on the app's pricing page and in the Apple App Store / Google Play listings. We don't dynamic-price based on who you are. Same plan, same price, every customer.

If we ever change pricing, existing subscribers keep their current rate for the duration of their billing cycle and we'll tell you what's changing before the next renewal.
