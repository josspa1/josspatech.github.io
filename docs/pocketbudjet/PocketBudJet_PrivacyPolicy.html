Privacy Policy

Effective Date: April 20, 2026 Developer: JosspaTech Contact: support@josspatech.com
The short version: We don't ask for your name, address, SSN, or bank credentials. Your financial data lives on your device by default — you decide if you want to back it up to a cloud provider you pick. A few transaction-level details are sent (anonymized) to power smart features like AI coaching, crowd benchmarks, and merchant categorization — the sections below list exactly what, when, and why.

1. Information We Collect

What we do NOT collect:

  • Your name, address, phone number, or email address (unless you choose to enter them for your own records)
  • Social Security numbers or government IDs
  • Bank account numbers, routing numbers, or card numbers
  • Passwords to any external services
  • Real-time bank or financial institution credentials
  • Precise location data
  • Device contacts or contact lists
  • Biometric data (Face ID / fingerprint are verified by your phone's OS — PocketBudJet never receives the biometric)
  • Persistent advertising identifiers tied to your financial data

What you enter into the app: Transactions, budgets, debts, goals, categories, and similar financial entries are stored in PocketBudJet's private app storage on your device. You can optionally enter a zip code and country to participate in crowd benchmarks (§9) — this is the only semi-identifying data we ask for, and it remains optional.

2. How Your Data Is Stored

On your device. Your data is kept in PocketBudJet's private app storage sandbox. On iOS, that storage is protected by Apple's Data Protection; on Android, by the platform's app-data isolation. Sensitive items like your PIN hash, biometric-lock flag, and any OAuth tokens you create are stored in the iOS Keychain or Android Keystore via expo-secure-store.

If you turn on backups or sync. You pick the cloud provider (iCloud, Google Drive, OneDrive, or Dropbox). Before anything leaves your device, the payload is compressed and encrypted with AES-256-CTR + HMAC-SHA256 using a key derived from a password only you know. JosspaTech has no copy of that password and cannot decrypt your backups.

On the network. All traffic to the services listed in §4, §9, and §10 is over TLS.

3. Camera and Receipt Scanning

Camera access is used for receipt scanning. By default, receipt images are processed with on-device OCR first (Apple Vision / Android ML Kit) — the image only leaves your phone if on-device OCR can't read it clearly, in which case a cloud OCR fallback is used for better accuracy.

Cloud OCR fallback (on by default). When and on-device OCR returns low confidence, the image is sent over TLS to OCR.Space for text extraction. OCR.Space does not retain uploads after processing. This is on by default and can be disabled in Settings → Advanced.

4. AI Financial Coach (Optional)

PocketBudJet's AI features come in two layers:

On-device AI (always on, free): Category prediction, anomaly detection, and spending forecasts run on your device. Nothing is transmitted for these.

AI Financial Coach (premium, opt-in): When you use a coach feature, a summary is sent over TLS to Anthropic, Inc. (the maker of the Claude AI) to generate personalized guidance. The payload includes:

  • Category spending breakdown (e.g. "Groceries: $380")
  • Top merchant names with aggregated totals (e.g. "Target: $210" — no dates, no individual transactions)
  • Monthly income, monthly spending, total debt, and calculated savings rate

What is NOT sent to Anthropic: Your name, email, phone, address, account numbers, card numbers, device identifiers, or individual transaction-level records. Anthropic does not retain customer API inputs for training (see Anthropic's Commercial Terms and privacy policy).

The AI Financial Coach is disabled by default and can be toggled in Settings. You can use PocketBudJet indefinitely without ever turning it on.

5. Bank Account Connectivity (Roadmap)

A future version of PocketBudJet will offer an optional feature to connect your bank accounts for transaction import via Plaid, Inc. This feature will:

  • Be entirely opt-in — disabled by default and enabled only by your explicit action
  • Use Plaid as the bank connectivity provider — JosspaTech will never see your bank credentials
  • Let you control sync frequency (manual-only is supported)
  • Show you every transaction for your review before anything is imported
  • Store Plaid access tokens on your device in Keychain/Keystore

This feature is not yet available. This policy will be updated with full details before it launches.

6. Voice Shortcuts

Voice shortcuts (Siri on iOS, Google Assistant / App Actions on Android) are opt-in and off by default. Voice audio is processed by Apple or Google under their respective privacy policies — not by JosspaTech. Only the resulting text command is used to create a transaction locally on your device.

7. Subscriptions and Payments

Purchases are processed entirely by Apple App Store or Google Play Store. JosspaTech does not collect, store, or process payment card information. Subscription status is verified through RevenueCat, which receives an anonymous app user ID — not your name or email.

8. Diagnostics and Crash Reports

PocketBudJet uses Firebase Crashlytics to collect anonymous crash diagnostics so we can find and fix bugs. Crash reports contain stack traces, device model, OS version, and app version. They do not contain your financial data. You can opt out in Settings → Data & Privacy.

9. Crowd Benchmarks and Merchant Intelligence

To make the app smarter for everyone, PocketBudJet contributes a small amount of anonymized data to a shared pool hosted by JosspaTech (PocketBase). You can turn this off any time in Settings → Data & Privacy.

What is contributed:

  • Merchant → category mappings (e.g. "Target → Groceries"). No amounts, dates, or identifiers.
  • Bucketed spending benchmarks by category, keyed to zip code and country, using coarse buckets so individual transactions are never reconstructable. No user ID, device ID, or IP.
  • Product intelligence: if you scan a barcode, the UPC is queried against public databases (upcitemdb.com, openfoodfacts.org). The UPC is all that's sent.

This pool powers features like "how does my Groceries spend compare to others in my zip?" and auto-categorization suggestions. JosspaTech may use the aggregated merchant database for commercial purposes, including licensing to third parties. Individual user data is not identifiable in the pool.

10. Other Network Services

  • Exchange rates (open.er-api.com): only currency pair codes (e.g. "USD→EUR") are sent. No amounts, no user data.
  • PC dashboard (optional): a local-only web server on your home network. Data stays inside your LAN.

11. Children's Privacy

PocketBudJet is not directed to children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child under 13 has provided information through the app, please contact us at support@josspatech.com.

12. Data Deletion

Because your financial data is stored on your device (and any backups you made to a cloud provider you control), you delete it by uninstalling the app and, if applicable, deleting your backups from your cloud provider. JosspaTech does not retain a copy of your financial data and has nothing to delete on our end. Anonymous crash reports and aggregated crowd-pool contributions cannot be linked back to you, so they cannot be individually withdrawn — but you can turn off future contributions at any time in Settings.

13. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will update the effective date when we do. Continued use of the App after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy. For material changes we will provide in-app notification.

14. Contact

Privacy questions or concerns: support@josspatech.com
