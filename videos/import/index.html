Slide 1

Finding the Download Option

Log into your bank's website. Look for "Download Transactions," "Export Activity," or "Transaction History." When your phone asks where to save the file, choose the PocketBudJet folder — specifically the Import subfolder inside it. It shows up right in your Save dialog on both iPhone and Android.

Slide 2

CSV is Best, But You Have Options

We recommend CSV—it's the most universal format and PocketBudJet's Smart Mapper handles it beautifully. If CSV isn't available, look for OFX or QFX formats. All work perfectly.

Slide 3

More Data = Better AI

Download as much history as your bank allows. Three months is the minimum—but six months or a year is even better. More data means smarter AI, better reports, and more useful trend analysis.

Slide 4

Save to PocketBudJet, Import Automatically

Save your bank file to the Import folder inside PocketBudJet on your phone. Next time you open the app, you'll see a green banner: "New files ready to import." One tap and you're done — no hunting through Downloads, no file picker. The app finds it for you and cleans up the file after a successful import.

Slide 5

Automatic Column Detection & Deduplication

The Smart Mapper is trained on dozens of real bank formats. It also detects overlapping date ranges and skips duplicates automatically. Nine times out of ten, no manual adjustment needed.

Slide 6

Snap Receipts With Your Camera

Don't have a bank file? No problem. Point your camera at any receipt and PocketBudJet's OCR reads the merchant, amount, date, and line items. Works with printed receipts and handwritten notes.

Slide 7

Feed a Stack, Walk Away

This is something we haven't seen in other budgeting apps. If you have a WiFi scanner at home with an automatic document feeder, connect directly. Feed receipts into the tray and walk away. Every receipt gets read and categorized.

Slide 8

Drag and Drop from Your Desktop

Do your banking on a computer? Open PocketBudJet's PC Web Dashboard by scanning a QR code. Download your bank file and drag it right into the import area. Syncs over local WiFi instantly.

Slide 9

Your Data, Your Way — 6 Formats

Export as CSV, XLSX, OFX, PDF, JSON, or encrypted backup. Every export goes to the PocketBudJet Export folder on your phone — the app tells you exactly where it is and keeps it there for 30 days. Share with your accountant, back up securely, or run a LAN server.
