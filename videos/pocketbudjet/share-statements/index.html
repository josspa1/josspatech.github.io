The Share Trick

The fastest way to get a bank statement into Pocket Budget isn’t downloading the file or emailing it to yourself. It’s the Share button that’s already in every bank app. Six taps from your bank’s home screen to a fully parsed statement in your budget.

Slide 2 of 8

Open Your Bank App

Open your bank’s app and tap into the account you want a statement from. Find the Statements section — usually under Accounts, Documents, or Activity. Pick the month you want.

Slide 3 of 8

Tap the Share Icon

Look for the Share icon — usually a square with an arrow pointing up, or three dots that lead to a Share menu. Some banks show it right next to each statement; others put it inside the open PDF view. Either way, it’s there.

Slide 4 of 8

The Share Sheet Appears

Your phone slides up the standard share sheet — the same one you see when you share a photo or a webpage. It shows every app on your phone that can accept a PDF.

Slide 5 of 8

Pick Pocket Budget

Tap the Pocket Budget icon. If you don’t see it in the first row, scroll right or tap More — once you pick it the first time, your phone remembers and pins it near the top.

Slide 6 of 8

Pocket Budget Takes Over

Pocket Budget opens directly to the Import screen with your statement already loaded. It auto-detects which bank the statement is from and starts reading the transactions immediately. No menu hunting. No file picker. The work is already underway by the time the screen finishes opening.

Slide 7 of 8

Review and Confirm

Pocket Budget shows you every transaction it found, color-coded by confidence. Green checkmarks mean it’s sure. A small flag means it would like a second pair of eyes. Tap any row to fix it. When you’re happy, tap Confirm.

Slide 8 of 8

Done

That’s the whole import. Your statement is now part of your budget — categorized, dated, and visible on your dashboard. The PDF itself never leaves your phone unless you choose to keep a copy in your own Cloud Backup.

Why Share Beats Every Other Import Method

1

Six Taps Total

From your bank’s home screen to a parsed statement: open account, open statements, pick month, tap Share, pick Pocket Budget, tap Confirm. That’s it.

2

No Downloads to Manage

Sharing skips your Downloads folder entirely. The PDF goes straight from your bank app to Pocket Budget, no file cleanup needed afterward.

3

Auto-Detects Your Bank

Pocket Budget recognizes the format from the very first page — Citizens, Chase, Bank of America, Wells Fargo, Capital One, smaller credit unions, and more. You don’t pick the bank; the app figures it out.

4

Works for Credit Card Statements Too

Same flow for any credit card statement — Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Discover, store cards. Pocket Budget knows the difference between a checking statement and a credit card statement and labels the transactions accordingly.

5

Password-Protected PDFs Supported

Some banks lock their PDFs with a password (often the last four of your account number, or your date of birth). Pocket Budget asks you for the password right inside the Import screen, unlocks it on your device, and never sends the password anywhere else.

6

Recurring Charges Auto-Linked

If a transaction matches a subscription you already track, Pocket Budget links them. Your Spotify charge from the statement won’t double up with the Spotify recurring you already added — it just confirms the recurring fired.

Why this is the privacy-friendly path

Pocket Budget never asks for your bank login. Sharing the statement gives Pocket Budget the data on the page — the same data you can already see — and nothing more. No ongoing access to your account, no permission to read future statements, no token sitting around. Each share is one file, one time. That’s it.

What if my bank app doesn’t have a Share button?

Most do, but a few hide it. If you can’t find one, download the PDF the normal way, save it to your Downloads folder, then tap the Plus button on the Transactions screen and tap Import. Pocket Budget will find it. Same outcome — just one more step. See the Downloads-folder workflow.
