Yes — **GitHub Pages is completely free** for public repositories. Here's exactly how to do it, no programming experience needed:

**Step 1 — Create a free GitHub account**
Go to github.com and sign up. It's free, takes 2 minutes.

**Step 2 — Create a new repository**
- Click the **+** in the top right → New repository
- Name it exactly: `josspatech.github.io` (this is the magic name that activates free hosting)
- Set it to **Public**
- Click Create repository

**Step 3 — Upload your files**
- Click **Add file → Upload files**
- Drag all 6 files from the zip into the browser window
- Click **Commit changes**

**Step 4 — Your site is live**
Within about 60 seconds your site will be live at:
- `https://josspatech.github.io` — company homepage
- `https://josspatech.github.io/PocketBudJet_PrivacyPolicy.html` — Privacy Policy
- `https://josspatech.github.io/PocketBudJet_TermsOfService.html` — Terms of Service
- `https://josspatech.github.io/PocketBudJet_EULA.html` — EULA

Those URLs are exactly what you paste into the App Store and Google Play when they ask for your Privacy Policy URL and Support URL.

**One thing to note:** GitHub Pages requires a public repository, which means anyone can see your HTML source code. That's completely fine for a public website — there's nothing sensitive in these files. Your actual app code would live in a separate private repository.

When you eventually buy **pocketbudjet.com**, you can point that domain to your GitHub Pages site for free too — so the URL becomes `pocketbudjet.com/privacy` instead of the github.io address. But the github.io URL works perfectly for beta and even for launch.
